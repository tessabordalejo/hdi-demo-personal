Program Areas

HDI is structured across three overlapping phases that trace the evolution of humanities research practices, infrastructure development, and governance concerns over time.

2021–2022

Foundations and FAIR Humanities Data

The initial SSHRC Partnership Development Grant focused on the problems and opportunities associated with Open and FAIR humanities data practices, including semantic publication workflows, repository infrastructure, and sustainable dissemination models for humanities and cultural heritage research.

This work examined how humanities "small data" could participate in emerging digital and computational ecosystems without sacrificing interpretive depth, disciplinary specificity, or long-standing scholarly practices.

2022–2027

Humanities Research Practices and Data Ecologies

This area of the project expanded into ethnographic, comparative, and historical study of humanities research practices, data use, and infrastructure design. Research focused primarily on North American contexts, with comparisons across Canadian and U.S. institutions.

Research in this area examines how humanities researchers, information professionals, and cultural institutions conceptualize, manage, and sustain research materials, digital collections, and scholarly infrastructure across diverse disciplinary and organizational settings.

2027–2029

Digital Research and Heritage Infrastructure, Governance, Stewardship, and Policy

This area of the project examines the governance, ethics, sustainability, and long-term stewardship of digital research and cultural heritage infrastructure.

Research focuses on institutional collaboration, infrastructure design, community authority, cultural data ethics, and policy development related to digital heritage and research infrastructure.
