Program Areas

HDI is structured across three overlapping phases that trace the evolution of humanities research practices, infrastructure development, and governance concerns over time.

2021–2022

Foundations and FAIR Humanities Data

The initial SSHRC Partnership Development Grant focused on Open and FAIR humanities data practices, semantic publication workflows, repository infrastructure, and sustainable dissemination models for humanities and cultural heritage research.

This phase examined how humanities “small data” can participate in digital and computational ecosystems without losing interpretive depth or disciplinary specificity.

2022–2027

Humanities Research Practices and Data Ecologies

This phase expands into ethnographic, comparative, and historical study of humanities research practices, data use, and infrastructure design across Canadian and U.S. institutions.

It examines how researchers and cultural institutions conceptualize, manage, and sustain research materials, digital collections, and scholarly infrastructure across disciplines.

2027–2029

Infrastructure, Governance, and Stewardship

This phase focuses on governance, ethics, sustainability, and long-term stewardship of digital research and cultural heritage infrastructure.

It explores institutional collaboration, infrastructure design, cultural data ethics, and policy development in the context of digital heritage systems and public memory.
