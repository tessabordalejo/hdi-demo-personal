Research Program

HDI uses a multi-method research approach combining ethnographic interviews and participant observation, comparative case analysis, systematic mapping reviews of the literature, historical research, and collaborative engagement with researchers and information professionals.

The project examines how humanities scholars conduct research across diverse disciplinary, institutional, and cultural heritage contexts, and how these practices shape—and are shaped by—the design, governance, and long-term stewardship of research and cultural heritage infrastructure.

Methods

  • Ethnographic interviews and participant observation
  • Comparative case studies across Canada and the United States
  • Historical analysis of humanities research practices
  • Systematic mapping of scholarly literature
  • Collaborative engagement with cultural heritage professionals

Key Areas of Inquiry

  • Humanities research practices and data cultures
  • Digital cultural heritage infrastructure
  • Governance, stewardship, and sustainability
  • Research data management and policy
  • Cultural data ethics and community authority
  • Digital transformation of the human cultural record
