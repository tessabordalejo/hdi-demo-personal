Research Program

HDI uses a multi-method research approach combining ethnographic interviews and participant observation, comparative case analysis, systematic mapping reviews of the literature, historical research, and collaborative engagement with researchers and information professionals.

The project examines how humanities scholars conduct research across diverse disciplinary, institutional, and cultural heritage contexts, and how these practices shape—and are shaped by—the collaborative design, governance, and long-term stewardship of research and cultural heritage infrastructure.

Research includes comparative fieldwork across universities and GLAM organizations (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, and Museums) in Canada and the United States, alongside research consultations with humanities scholars, archivists, librarians, curators, technologists, and policy actors working across diverse research and cultural heritage settings.

This work contributes to scholarly dialogue on

  • Humanities research practices and data cultures
  • Digital cultural heritage infrastructure
  • Governance, stewardship, and sustainability of digital collections
  • Research data management and policy
  • Cultural data ethics and community authority
  • Digital transformation of the human cultural record
