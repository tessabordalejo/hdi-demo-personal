This poster provides a snapshot of the Humanities Data Inquiry (HDI), a new community of practice exploring data issues in Humanities and Cultural Heritage (HCH) research. The last decade has seen great advances in the development of infrastructure, tools, and principles for the collection, storage, discovery, and dissemination of research data, and investment in a robust and rapidly emerging Open and FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable) research data ecosystem.
